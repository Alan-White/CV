Rope access technician (abseiler) and non-destructive tester (inspector)

  • Abseiler/inspector. Between 1999 and 2016 I was working mainly in the offshore oil and gas industry. I was subcontracted by larger companies to supervise and carry out inspection work on pipelines and structures. I was a level 3 rope access supervisor and was responsible for a team of three or four abseilers carrying out work at height on oil platforms offshore or on bridges etc onshore. I assessed the worksite, completed a written rescue plan, brief the team and ensured the work is carried out safely. This often involved liaising with a control room and rescue boat via radio. If a rescue had been necessary, I would have completed the rescue myself or directed the other team members.
  • Abseiler. I passed the level 2 course in 2000 and the level 3 course in 2003. After which I was leading teams on various jobs around the world.
  • Inspector. I was qualified in ultrasonic, magnetic particle and dye penetrant inspection methods. I was also qualified to assist a radiographer.
  • Places worked: North Sea (Offshore Holland and Scotland), Caspian Sea (offshore Azerbaijan), Gulf of Mexico (offshore Texas), Louisiana, Atlantic (onboard a tanker from North Carolina to Virgin Islands), Norway, Angola (offshore), Mediteranean (offshore Spain) and France.
